This study examined whether gender differences in the perception of pain facial expressions arise from differences in observers’ mental representations. Using reverse correlation and machine-learning analyses, the results show that mental representations of pain are largely similar for male and female faces, although observers perceive greater pain and sadness intensity in female pain expressions.
